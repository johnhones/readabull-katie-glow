Marketing Strategy
Step 1: Correct Your Category Placement

The category your book sits in determines which readers find it and what revenue is possible. Sci fi AI romance generates $52 a month on average across 15 books. Dark sci fi romance generates $5,756 a month on average across 14 books. That difference is entirely down to category placement. The right categories are not just labels: they are the sales mechanism.

✓
Move into sci fi dystopian categories first
Sci fi dystopian is a high-traffic, well-established browse path with a proven buyer base. The average BSR in this category is 436,849, which puts it within realistic reach in the near term. Email KDP support via Author Central and request Science Fiction › Dystopian as a category. This is the faster route to organic visibility while reviews are still building.
✓
Add dark sci fi romance as a second category
Dark sci fi romance is where the revenue ceiling lives. The top books in this niche earn $3,058 to over $6,583 a month. Request this category alongside sci fi dystopian so the book benefits from both browse paths simultaneously. The average price in dark sci fi romance is $6.77, and at $5.99 this listing is priced competitively to convert.
✓
Update your 7 KDP keyword fields to match the new categories
KDP backend keywords must align with the categories you are targeting. Once categories are corrected, replace any generic or mismatched keywords with dark sci fi romance, dystopian romance, and simulation romance terms. The Readerbull Recommended panel in this audit contains the priority terms. Copy them directly into your 7 keyword fields in KDP.
✓
Rewrite your description to speak to dark romance readers
Dark romance readers want emotional intensity, high stakes, and a relationship that breaks the rules. Your first two sentences need to put the reader inside that tension before they scroll. If your opening line describes the world-building before the emotional hook, rewrite it. Lead with the reader's desire, not the premise.
↓
Step 2: Build to 15 Reviews Before Running Ads

With 2 ratings and reviews and a 5.0 star average, the book is delivering for readers. Reaching 15 reviews gives the listing the social proof it needs to convert ad traffic into sales. At 15 reviews this title is a credible, competitive entry in dark sci fi romance. The more reviews built from there, the stronger the conversion rate becomes. Romance readers on ARC platforms are highly active and leave detailed reviews.

A 5.0 star average from 2 reviews is a strong foundation. Every additional genuine review locks in that quality signal and makes the listing more convincing to new readers. The goal is not just quantity: it is consistent quality at volume.
✓
Use an ARC service to reach 20 to 30 reviews quickly
BookSirens, Hidden Gems, and NetGalley connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. These are legitimate, Amazon-compliant services. The dark romance readership on these platforms is large and highly engaged. A well-presented ARC campaign for this title can generate 20 to 40 reviews within 4 to 6 weeks.
✓
Add a review request inside the book
Place a natural, warm request mid-book and at the back. Many readers who genuinely enjoyed the book simply never think to leave a review without a prompt. Do not ask for a specific star rating: Amazon's terms prohibit this. A genuine ask yields genuine responses, and every future sale from this point forward has a higher chance of generating a review.
✓
Reach dark romance and sci fi communities directly
BookTok and BookTwt have thriving dark romance communities. Reddit's r/DarkRomance and r/scifiromance have tens of thousands of active readers. Facebook groups for dark romance and sci fi romance readers are precisely the audience this book is written for. A sincere, authentic pitch to these communities can generate reviews and early word-of-mouth simultaneously.
↓
Step 3: Launch Amazon Ads for Both Formats

This is the action that moves the Sales/mo bar. Reviews get the listing ready. Ads move the number. Every book earning over $200 a month in dark sci fi romance is running ads. The Tether Protocol earns $441 a month with 21 reviews. That is the 90-day target: reachable at 15 reviews with a focused Sponsored Products campaign. The paperback at $14.99 generates a higher royalty per sale and is the primary ad target. The Kindle at $5.99 supports KU reads and conversion volume.

✓
Start with Sponsored Products at $5 to $7 a day
Target keyword phrases: “dark sci fi romance,” “sci fi dystopian romance,” and “simulation romance books.” These reach buyers with paid-book intent. Run for 14 days before reviewing results. Do not increase budget until the campaign shows a profitable ACoS. Pause any keyword without a sale after 14 days.
✓
Add Sponsored Display ads on competitor ASINs
Target the product pages of Signal Lost and The Tether Protocol. Buyers browsing those pages are already in a dark sci fi romance buying mindset. A well-placed display ad intercepts that intent directly and converts at a higher rate than keyword-only campaigns because the reader is already primed to buy.
✓
Target ACoS below 40% for the paperback
At $14.99 paperback, the royalty is approximately $4.84. A profitable ACoS means spending less than $4.84 per sale in ad spend. Scale only what converts consistently. The Tether Protocol benchmark at 21 reviews and $441 a month is a realistic 90-day target once ads are live and categories are corrected.

Step 4: Add A+ Content and Optimize Your Listing

A+ Content is free to create in KDP and is one of the most effective listing upgrades available to authors. It adds branded images, a comparison module, and expanded text sections below the description. Dark romance buyers respond strongly to visual storytelling. A listing with A+ Content converts at a measurably higher rate than one without it. With the cover already delivering visually, A+ Content is the next logical step.

✓
Build A+ Content in Author Central
Log in to Amazon Author Central and navigate to the A+ Content tool. Use the cover image and any additional graphics to create a branded module below the description. At minimum, add a text-and-image row that reinforces the dark romance hook and positions the book as part of a series. This takes under two hours and is live within 24 to 48 hours of submission.
✓
Ensure your series branding is visible on the listing
If this is the first book in a series, the series name should appear in the subtitle field and in the A+ Content module. Series branding is a conversion signal for dark romance readers: it tells them there is more to read and raises the value of the purchase. A reader who knows this is Book One is more likely to buy than one who assumes it is a standalone.

Book Portfolio Opportunity
A Series Compounds Discoverability and Revenue
Two books by the same author in the same series cross-sell each other automatically. Amazon’s “customers also bought” system means every reader who buys Book One is shown Book Two. Each new title adds a new keyword footprint, a new category placement, and a new set of search rankings. Series readers are also the most loyal buyers on the platform.
1
Book Two: Continue the Simulation Series
Dark romance readers finish a book and immediately search for the next. A series continuation captures that impulse at the peak of engagement. Signal Lost is evidence that readers in this niche follow a series: 82 reviews and a top-performing BSR require sustained reader loyalty. A second title in the Beyond the Simulation series starts with a warm audience already primed to buy.
2
A companion novella at a lower price point
A shorter companion story at $2.99 to $3.99 serves as a series entry point for price-sensitive readers. Once inside the world, those readers purchase full-price books at a high rate. Novellas also generate their own reviews, keywords, and category placements, expanding the total search footprint without requiring a full-length manuscript.
3
A standalone dark sci fi romance in an adjacent sub-niche
The simulation concept has clear crossover into dark romance sub-niches including captive romance, morally grey heroes, and enemies-to-lovers. A standalone title in one of these sub-niches reaches a new set of search terms while cross-selling back to the existing catalogue. Authors with two or more titles in the same genre consistently outperform single-title authors at equivalent review counts.

Why Series Revenue Compounds

Amazon’s also-bought and frequently-viewed-together algorithms treat series books as a cluster. Every sale of Book One is a potential sale of Book Two. Every review on Book Two increases the visibility of Book One. The compounding effect means a two-book series earning $441 a month per title does not earn $882 a month combined: it earns significantly more because each book amplifies the other’s organic ranking.
Focus on Steps 1 to 4 first. Correct categories, build to 15 reviews, and get ads running before starting the next title. Once the first book is earning consistently, a second title compounds that income without starting from zero.
